Introduction

The growing demand for automated vehicle identification systems has highlighted the importance of efficient license plate detection. Such systems play a critical role in various applications, including law enforcement for tracking stolen vehicles, parking management for efficient space utilization, and traffic monitoring for maintaining road safety [1]. This project aims to develop a license plate detection system.



Approach

To achieve effective license plate detection, we will utilize a combination of image processing techniques and deep learning algorithms. The approach will involve:

  • Data Collection: We will compile a dataset of images containing vehicles with clearly visible license plates, sourced from publicly available datasets, such as Kaggle [2] or Dataset Ninja [3]. The dataset will include state and pictures, examples are U.S. license plates include Car License Plate Dataset [4] and US License Plates [5].
  • Preprocessing: The images will undergo several preprocessing steps to enhance quality and ensure consistency, including:
    • Noise Reduction: Techniques such as Gaussian blur and median filtering will be employed to eliminate unwanted artifacts and enhance clarity.
    • Color Space Transformation: Images will be converted to color spaces like HSV or LAB to improve the visibility of license plates under different lighting conditions.
    • Edge Detection Techniques: To further highlight the boundaries of the license plates and characters, edge detection methods such as the Canny edge detector will be applied.
    • Windowing Around Letters: A sliding window technique will be used to focus specifically on regions containing potential characters.
  • Detection Model: We will implement a deep learning-based object detection architecture, specifically selecting YOLOv8 [6] due to its balance between speed and accuracy for real-time applications.
  • Model Evaluation: The model's performance will be evaluated primarily on its precision in detecting the actual license number plate.


Experiments and Results

The experiments will be structured as follows:

  • Input: The input will be photos of vehicle or license plates taken from datasets.
  • Dataset Preparation: Ensure a diverse range of license plate designs and conditions in our dataset, encompassing various lighting and angle scenarios.
  • Implementation: The detection algorithm will be implemented in Python.
  • Output: The output would be the license plate number.
  • Success Metrics: Success will be defined as achieving a detection accuracy above 70% on the test dataset. The experimental setup will involve splitting the dataset into training, validation, and test sets.
  • Model Optimization: : Data augmentation techniques, such as rotation, scaling, and color adjustment, will be employed to improve model robustness. We will experiment with different model architectures and hyperparameters to identify the best-performing configuration.
